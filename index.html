Recipe #1-ghorme Sabzi

Ghormeh sabzi is deliciously savory and loaded with the flavors of several different green herbs. It's traditionally served atop white rice (polow). You can also serve it with lavash bread.

  • Time: 3 hrs 9 mins
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients Quantity
large onion one
canola oil 1/4 cup
ground turmeric 1 tea spoon
boneless chuck roast 1/5pounds
finely chopped spinach 1/5 cups
finely chopped green onions (green part only) 1 cup
finely chopped Italian flat-leaf parsley 0/5 cup
inely chopped cilantro 1/4 cup
finely chopped chives 1/4 cup
finely chopped fenugreek leaves 1/4 cup
cups water, or more as needed 1/5 cups
salt and ground black pepper to taste
lemon, juiced 1
dried Persian limes (limoo amani) 1 or more to taste
can red kidney beans, drained and rinsed 1 (15 ounce)


Step 1: Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add onion; cook and stir until deep golden brown, 10 to 15 minutes. Stir in turmeric for 1 to 2 minutes. Add chuck cubes; cook until coated in turmeric and browned on all sides, 8 to 10 minutes.

Step 2:Heat remaining 2 tablespoons oil in a separate pot over medium heat. Add spinach, green onions, parsley, cilantro, chives, and fenugreek leaves; cook and stir until deep dark green in color, 5 to 10 minutes.

Step 3:Stir spinach mixture into the onion and chuck mixture. Pour in enough water to create a slurry consistency. Season with salt and pepper. Pour in lemon juice. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer stew until greens soften, about 1 hour.

Step 4:Pierce dried limes with a fork and add to the stew. Continue simmering until chuck is tender, 30 minutes to 1 hour. Stir in red kidney beans. Cook until flavors combine, about 30 minutes. Discard dried limes before serving.

Recipe #2-Bolani

Bolani are stuffed Afghani flatbreads, baked or fried. Dr Rupy stuffs the simple dough with sweet potato and spinach for a flavourful vegetarian meal or party food that costs under £1 per portion.

  • Time: 2 hrs
  • Serves: 2
Ingredients Quantity
For the dough
chapati flour 250 gr
For the filling
sweet potato 1 large
olive oil for fry
leek, thinly sliced 1 large
garlic cloves crushed or grated
frozen spinach, defrosted 200 gr
pinch chilli flakes
black pepper freshly ground
For the sauce
Greek yoghurt, or plant-based yoghurt 200 gr
lemon, juice only 1/2
garlic clove 1 crushed

Step 1:To a large bowl, add the water, oil, salt, and yeast. Stir, and then add the flour. Mix well.

Step 2:Then knead the dough* for 3 minutes, or until soft and elastic. Add a little more flour as needed if the dough feels too sticky to work with. Cover the bowl with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes at room temperature while preparing the filling

Step 3:Cook the potatoes in a large pot of boiling water for 15 minutes, or until the potatoes are fully cooked through. Then drain.

Step 4:To a large bowl, add the drained potatoes, green onion, cilantro, chili, ground coriander, salt, turmeric, and pepper. Use a fork or potato masher to mash everything. It's okay if a few lumps remain.

Step 5:Cut the dough into 8 pieces.

Step 6:Roll each piece into a ball with your hands. Dust each ball with flour.

Step 7:Sprinkle some flour on your working surface and use a rolling pin to roll each ball into a thin circle with a diameter of roughly 20 - 22 cm. We're aiming for a thickness similar to that of a tortilla. The thinner the better, but not so thin that it will rip if handled.

Step 8:Before adding the filling, gently lift the rolled-out dough and heavily re-flour your working surface. Spread ⅓ of a cup of the potato filling over half of the dough, leaving a ½ inch (1 cm) border along the edge. Dip your fingers in water and spread the water along this edge, only on one half of the circle.

Step 9:Fold the dough in half, over the filling, to create a half-moon shape. Press out all the air bubbles, then pinch the edges with your fingers to fully seal the bolani.

Step 10:Add 1 tsp (5 mL) of oil to a large pan over medium-high heat. When the oil is hot, add the bolani to the pan. Let cook for 2 - 3 minutes or until the bottom is golden. Meanwhile, use a silicone pastry brush to brush some more oil on top of the bolani.

Step 11:When the bottom is golden, flip, and let cook on the other side until golden. Then transfer to a cooling rack.

Step 12:Cut the bolani in triangles and serve with green chutney. Enjoy!

Recipe #3-Kabuli Palaw

Kabuli Pulao is an Afghan rice dish with lamb, raisins and carrots. The spice combination of cardamom, garam masala and ground coriander gives it a unique taste.

  • Time: 2 hrs and 20 min
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients Quantity
lamb 300 gr
chiken meat 300 gr
onions 2
carrots 300 gr
basmati rice 500 gr
raisins 100 gr
garam masala 1 teaspoon
chopped almonds 80 gr
ground cardamom 1 teaspoon
coriander 1/2 tsp
salt-oil-pepper and sugar -
Step 1:Rinse the rice until the draining water is clear. Afterward, let the rice swell in the water for about 2 hours.

Step 2:Cut the onions into small cubes. The meat can be freed from sinews and fat and then cut into bite-sized pieces. Sweat the onions in a hot pan in a little oil. Then add the meat and fry it until brown.

Step 3:In the meantime you can mix the spices with about 500ml of warm water to a spice broth. With this, you extinguish the meat in the pan. Let everything come to the boil and cook the meat for about 1 hour with a closed lid and low heat.

Step 4:While waiting for the meat, you can first cut or grate the carrots into small, very thin sticks.

Step 5:Heat some oil in a large pan and fry the raisins briefly until they swell. Take them out and then fry the carrot sticks in the same oil together with 1 tablespoon of twitch for about 3-4 minutes. Remove the carrot sticks and roast the chopped almonds briefly.

Step 6: As soon as the meat is cooked, take it out of the pan and put it in the oven while it is still warm. Pour the remaining meat stock into a large pot and add the rice. Now let the rice simmer on low heat until all the liquid is absorbed.

Step 7:When the liquid has been absorbed, you can first use a wooden spoon to pierce small chimneys in the rice so that the steam can escape. Now place the warm meat on top and cover it with the carrots, raisins and almonds. Leave everything to simmer for another 30 minutes at very low heat with the lid closed (wrap a clean kitchen towel around it to absorb the steam).

Step 8:To serve, first put the meat on a plate and then layer the rice on top. Finally, add the carrots, almonds and raisins to the rice and serve hot.
